Help

How a scan works

Pick who's watching and press Scan. jellylook reads their recent watch history from Jellystat, sends one request to your chosen AI provider for a full batch of suggestions, then adds posters and ratings from TMDb and OMDb. Sorting, filtering and paging never trigger another AI call.

What the match % means

It's the AI's own estimate of how close a title sits to your recent viewing, from 0 to 100. Directionally useful, not science.

"Because you watched"

Each suggestion names the specific title in your history that inspired it, so you can see the connection at a glance.

The .env knobs

LLM_PROVIDER switches between anthropic, openai, google, openwebui and ollama (with LLM_MODEL for the model name). HISTORY_SOURCE switches between jellystat and jellyfin. RECS_PER_SCAN, PER_PAGE and RETENTION_DAYS control batch size, paging and how long results are kept. Restart the container after changing .env.

Troubleshooting

Source unreachable — a scan that fails immediately usually means Jellystat or Jellyfin can't be reached or the API key is wrong. Check the container logs.
Seerr down — cards still render; the Add button is disabled until Seerr answers again.
OMDb limit — the free key allows 1000 lookups a day. The cache keeps re-scans nearly free; today's count shows in Settings.
